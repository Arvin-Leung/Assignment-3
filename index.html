What Were His Contributions to The History of Computer Science?

In 1989, Tim Berners-Lee was the one who invented the World Wide Web and laid the fundamental technologies of HTML, URL, and HTTPs. The World Wide Web allows people like you and me to visit websites and create them. Without him, this website would not exist at all. Think about websites like Amazon, Apple, Youtube, and Facebook all use the World Wide Web to display and store their websites. Tim Berners-Lee by far is one of the biggest contributors to the History of Computer Science.


Biography About His Life

Tim Berners-Lee was born on June 8, 1955, London, England. Not much is said about his early life, but we know that Tim was fascinated by technology and computers as both of his parents worked on the Ferranti Mark 1. In 1976, he would graduate from Oxford University and designed computer software for 2 years at a company called Plessey Telecommunications. In 1989, Tim Berners-Lee would begin to plan out a hyper text document system which uses the internet, this would become html. His initial reason for creating this was to allow researchers to share their results without the need of exchanging email constantly. Between october 1990 and the summer of 1991, The first application would be developed, which was the laboratory’s telephone directory. From 1991 and 1993, he would spread the word of the web he had created. In 2004, Tim Berners Lee would be knighted by Queen Elizabeth for his grand contribution to the creation of the internet. In 2012, Tim became the Co-founder of Open Data institute, which hopes to inspire people to innovate more with data.

Tim Berners-Lee’s Opinion on today’s Web

Today, the internet has changed a lot but also harms many people’s lives. Tim Berners-Lee wanted the internet to be a place where people could work together and share information easily. The thing is that in our modern era, people use the internet to bully people and it even affects politics. Internet trolls and hackers will use the internet only to make other people’s days worse. This was not what Tim Berners-Lee wanted when he created the web. Tim Berners-Lee has even mentioned that he dreads the thing he has created and believes that it harms more people than it helps like some kinda Frankenstein monster.
